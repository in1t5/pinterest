Teacher opens a
spreadsheet in January,
retires fifteen years early.

By the numbers · with our free financial ledger
She didn't earn more. She didn't time the market. She opened a financial ledger on the first of January, wrote down every asset and every liability, and did it again the following month. Forty-two months later she had paid off the car, the overdraft, and the instinct to ignore her balance sheet.
The ledger does one thing: it replaces the vague anxiety of not-knowing with the precise discomfort of knowing exactly. That discomfort is productive. It nudges spending before the month ends rather than explaining it after.
Net worth rose from €−12K to €480K over fifteen quiet years. Monthly contributions averaged €2,200. Compounding did the rest — silently, without any fanfare, exactly as advertised.
The ledger didn't make me rich. It made me honest.
